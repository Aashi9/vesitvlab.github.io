Procedure: (Transmission(Attenuation) Characteristics of Optical Fiber)


Part A: (To study Atenuation in Optical Fiber due to various Mechanisms)

In this experiment we are observing the effect of wavelength of input signal on attenuation in Fiber.

  1. Vary the wavelength(in μm) from 0.4μm to 2μm with incremental steps of 0.1μm with the help of the wavelength slider to obtain the attenuation for the mechanisms.
  2. Select one wavelength and then click the calculate button in order to calculate the values and the value is obtained as one of the row as output in the table given.
  3. After calculating all the values for the given input wavelengths, click on the plot button to plot the graphs.
  4. Observe the variation of attenuation with wavelength for a particular mechanism.
  5. Write down the Result and Conclusion observed, in the area given below.

    6. NOTE: If due to some reasons there occurs any mistake while taking the readings you can click on the reset button in order to start taking the readings again.

Part B: (To study effect of change in Length on Output Power of Optical Fiber)

In this experiment we are observing the effect of length of propagation on attenuation in Fiber.

  1. In this experiment, the input power is selected as 1mw.
  2. First select the length of Fiber(in km) from 0.5km to 10km with incremental steps of 500m with the help of the slider given.
  3. After Selecting one value from the slider click on the calculate button in order to calculate the output Power and that value is added in a row of the table.
  4. After varying all the length values click on the plot button to plot the graph.
  5. Observe the variation of attenuation with length.
  6. Write down the Result and Conclusion observed, in the area given below.

    7. NOTE: If due to some reasons there occurs any mistake while taking the readings you can click on the reset button in order to start taking the readings again.
